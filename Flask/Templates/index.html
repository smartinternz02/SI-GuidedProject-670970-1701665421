About

The ability to accurately classify scenes in images is fundamental in various applications, ranging from autonomous navigation to image organization and augmented reality. In this project, we explore a novel approach to improve scene classification accuracy by leveraging the benefits of virtual images and real-life photographs. By training a convolutional neural network (CNN) model on virtual images generated from 3D virtual worlds and then fine-tuning it with real images, we aim to investigate the potential of transferring knowledge from simulation to reality. The dataset used in this project consists of two sets: virtual images and real images, both labeled with six scene categories. We employ the VGG16 model, a deep CNN renowned for its powerful feature extraction capabilities. This project aims to uncover whether learning from virtual data can enhance the accuracy and generalization of scene classification in real-world scenarios, ultimately contributing to advancements in computer vision and image analysis.
